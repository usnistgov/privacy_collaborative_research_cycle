Below we give proper credit to the teams who have contributed to our data archive, along with summary results we've learned about the algorithms so far. For recent progress analyzing what these results might mean, check out our Results Blog.

Archive Contributors Leaderboard:

Teams are ranked first by total number files submitted, then number of algorithms contributed to the project. Because the goal of this project is to collect data to support a better understanding of deidentification algorithms, its expected that teams will explore a variety of algorithms (and parameter configurations) with a range of utility performance scores. To illustrate this, the score range is given for each team, but this is not factored in the archive contributor ranking.

Publicly Verifiable Differential Privacy Leaderboard:

TBA: This leaderboard will operate similarly to the leaderboards from the NIST Differential Privacy Synthetic Data Challenges. Contributors attesting publicly verifiable differential privacy who wish to appear on this leaderboard are requested to submit two data sets using the national target data with the "simpler 21 feature-set" (all features except INDP, [and weights]). One data set should satisfy epsilon = 1, and the other should satisfy epsilon = 10, both with delta <= 10-5-.
